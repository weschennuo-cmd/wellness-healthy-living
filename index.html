Creator Direction

Wellness & Healthy Living

Balanced routines. Recovery moments. Everyday wellbeing.

Story first, product second.

Build from a real moment, show the before-and-after change, then let POCOCO appear as the atmosphere that helps the story make sense.

01

Story Framework

Use this as a flexible logic path. Creators do not need to copy every word, but the story should have a clear emotional reason.

01

Real Moment

Frame POCOCO inside a broader lifestyle system: training recovery, healthy evening habits, travel reset, nutrition prep, or a routine that protects energy.

02

Before

Show the lifestyle friction: the body is tired, the room still feels active, the creator needs recovery but the environment does not support that shift.

03

POCOCO Scene

Use the projection as one layer in the wellness ritual: hydration, stretching, reading, breathwork, skincare, or quiet time after a demanding day.

04

After

Show the routine feeling more complete: body slows down, room changes, recovery feels intentional, and wellness becomes something built through environment.

05

Natural CTA

Speak to people who already invest in habits. The CTA should feel like a routine upgrade, not a miracle claim.

Wellness creators should make POCOCO part of a lifestyle pattern. It works best when the product supports a larger system of healthy habits.
02

Message Mention

Keep the product integrated into the routine. Mention only the benefits that help explain the before-and-after lifestyle shift.

PRT

Psychological Restoration Theory

Use the projected universe as a restorative environment cue: a visual shift that can help the story move from pressure, noise, or emotional load into a calmer state.

ART

Attention Restoration Theory

Show soft fascination: visuals that hold attention gently without asking the audience to keep scrolling, reading, or working harder.

CRT

Conditioned Restoration Theory

Connect repeated rituals with the same calming atmosphere so the projection becomes a familiar cue for bedtime, reset, focus, or connection.

Recovery atmosphere

Show how lighting and room mood can support the transition from performance mode to recovery mode.

Habit stacking

Pair POCOCO with an existing habit: stretching, tea, skincare, reading, meditation, or a post-workout routine.

Portable ritual

Cordless use can support wellness moments in different spaces, including bedroom, living room, or travel setup.

Screen-free evening

Position the projection as a visual alternative when the creator wants to reduce late-night scrolling.

Full-room change

Use wide shots to show that the product changes the environment, not just one corner of the ceiling.

Careful claim

Avoid suggesting POCOCO alone improves health outcomes. It is part of an intentional environment.

Creative starting points

Scenes that can carry the story

Pick the scenes that match your creator style and audience. The product should feel naturally connected to the moment.

  • Post-workout recovery
  • Healthy evening routine
  • Travel recovery setup
  • High-performance lifestyle wind-down
  • Morning vs evening routine contrast
